No Tomorrow Brewing Co. is my homebrewing operation — small-batch, experimental, brewed like there's no tomorrow. It's also an excuse to combine the two things I apparently can't stop doing: making beer and building dashboards. The result lives at [notomorrowbrewing.com](https://notomorrowbrewing.com).

The site is styled like a space mission, and it's organized into three stations:

## The Taproom

The front door: what's on tap right now, plus the operation's vital signs — batches brewed, taps pouring, and the average ABV across the lineup.

</figure>

## Mission Control

The fun part. Whatever is in the fermenter reports live telemetry to the site: current gravity against target, temperature and its trend, estimated ABV, the gravity drop rate, and attenuation progress from original gravity to final. It's the difference between wondering how the beer is doing and checking the instruments.

<figure>
<img src="/images/ntb/mission-control.webp" alt="Mission Control dashboard for an actively fermenting American IPA showing gravity, temperature, estimated ABV, drop rate, and attenuation progress" loading="lazy">
<figcaption>Mission Control: live readings from the active fermentation.</figcaption>
</figure>

## The Library

Every batch gets a permanent record: style, hop profile, and its full fermentation history — thousands of gravity and temperature readings charted from pitch to package. Old batches become reference material for new ones.
